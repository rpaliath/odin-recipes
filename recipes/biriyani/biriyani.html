Chicken Biriyani

Ingradients

  • Chicken - 1 kg
  • Basmati Rice - 500 gm
  • Yogurt - 200 gm
  • Onions - 2 large, thinly sliced
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium, chopped
  • Garlic Paste - 2 tbsp
  • Ginger Paste - 2 tbsp
  • Biriyani Masala - 3 tbsp
  • Saffron - a few strands soaked in warm milk
  • Fresh Coriander and Mint Leaves - a handful each, chopped
  • Ghee or Oil - 4 tbsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water - as needed

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt,ginger and garlic paste, biriyani masala, and salt. Let it sit for at least 1 hour.

  2. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice for 30 minutes, then drain.

  3. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt and the soaked rice. Cook until the rice is 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.

  4. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat ghee or oil. Fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until they soften.

  5. Add the marinated chicken to the pan. Cook until the chicken is fully cooked and the oil starts to separate from the masala.

  6. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken in the pan. Sprinkle the saffron milk, chopped coriander, and mint leaves on top over the pan with a tight-fitting lid. Cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes to allow the flavors to blend and the rice to cook.

Serving

Gently fluff the biriyani before serving. Serve hot with raita or a side salad.
