Masala Dosa

Description

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish consisting of a thin, crispy crepe made from fermented rice and urad dal batter, filled with
a spiced potato mixture. The dosa is cooked on a griddle until golden and crispy, It is usually served with coconut chutney and sambar,
a lentil-based vegetable stew.

Ingredients

For Dosa Batter:

  • Rice - 2 cups
  • Urad Dal (split black gram) - 1/2 cup
  • Fenugreek Seeds - 1 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water - as needed

For Potato Filling:

  • Potatoes - 4 medium, boiled and mashed
  • Onion - 1 large, finely chopped
  • Green Chilies - 2, finely chopped
  • Ginger - 1 inch piece, grated
  • Mustard Seeds - 1 tsp
  • Curry Leaves - a few
  • Turmeric Powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil - 2 tbsp

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately under cold water. Soak them in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.

  2. Drain the soaked ingredients. Grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds to a smooth batter, adding water as needed. Then grind the rice to a slightly coarse batter.

  3. Mix both batters together in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well. Cover and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight.

  4. For the potato filling, heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves, chopped onions, green chilies, and grated ginger. Saut√© until onions are translucent.

  5. Add turmeric powder and salt. Mix well. Add the mashed potatoes and combine everything thoroughly. Cook for a few minutes, then remove from heat and set aside.

  6. Heat a non-stick griddle or tawa over medium heat. Pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the center of the griddle and spread it out in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.

  7. Drizzle a little oil around the edges of the dosa. Cook until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Flip if desired, but it's optional.

  8. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the dosa. Fold the dosa over the filling to form a semi-circle or roll it up.


Serving

Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
